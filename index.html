🦉 How to File Taxes

✅ Gather Your Documents

How to collect your income info:
  • Gather all W-2 forms from employers.
  • Collect 1099 forms for freelance, interest, or dividends.
  • Keep records of side gigs, rental income, or tips.

✅ Understand Your W-2

How to read your W-2:
  • Box 1 – Total wages you earned.
  • Box 2 – Federal tax withheld.
  • Box 12 – Codes for retirement, deductions, or other benefits.
  • Compare totals with your pay stubs to ensure accuracy.

✅ Track Interest & Dividends

How to collect interest income:
  • Look for 1099-INT for bank interest.
  • 1099-DIV for dividends from stocks or mutual funds.
  • Include small earnings from savings or accounts to avoid underreporting.

✅ Organize Deductions

How to collect deductible expenses:
  • Keep receipts for charitable donations.
  • Track medical expenses if itemizing.
  • Save education or tuition receipts for credits.

✅ Step-by-Step 1040 Filing

🦉 How to add up your income (Lines 4–8)

  • Line 4 – Add any interest you earned from banks or accounts.
  • Line 5 – Include dividends from stocks or other investments.
  • Line 6 – Report any IRA distributions or pension payments.
  • Line 7 – Include Social Security benefits if applicable.
  • Line 8 – Add other income, such as gambling winnings, freelance work, or tips.
Extra Tips:
  • Check all forms carefully (1099-INT, 1099-DIV, etc.) to avoid mistakes.
  • Include any side jobs or occasional work, no matter how small.
  • Add numbers accurately to prevent delays or errors on your return.
  • Think of it like putting together pieces of a puzzle — you need each one!

🦉 How to subtract deductions

  • Choose between the standard deduction or itemized deductions.
  • Include eligible education expenses for deductions.
  • Don’t forget charitable donations.
Extra Tips: Deductions reduce your taxable income, which can lower the amount of tax you owe. Always pick the larger option: standard or itemized.

🦉 How to apply tax credits

  • Claim the child tax credit if you qualify.
  • Include the earned income credit if eligible.
  • Use education credits, such as the American Opportunity Credit.
Extra Tips: Credits reduce your tax bill directly. Make sure to claim all credits you’re eligible for.

🦉 How to adjust your income (Line 9)

  • Include deductions for student loan interest.
  • Add contributions to an IRA.
  • Subtract self-employment expenses if applicable.
Extra Tips:
  • Adjustments reduce your total income, which can lower your taxes.
  • Don’t skip adjustments—they can save you money!

🦉 Calculate your Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)

Your AGI is your total income minus adjustments. Keep it handy — it’s needed for deductions, credits, and figuring taxes owed.

Extra Tips: Knowing your AGI helps you plan your deductions and estimate your tax.

🦉 Choose deductions (Lines 11–12)

  • Line 11 – Choose the standard deduction or itemized deduction using Schedule A.
  • Line 12 – Taxable income = AGI minus deductions.
Extra Tips:
  • Use the larger deduction to lower your tax.
  • Every dollar counts — even small deductions can help!

🦉 Calculate your tax (Lines 13–15)

  • Line 13 – Tax owed based on your taxable income.
  • Schedule 2 – Add any additional taxes, like self-employment tax or AMT.
  • Line 15 – Total tax = base tax + additional taxes.
Extra Tips: Double-check your math. Use tax tables or software to ensure accuracy.

🦉 Apply credits and payments (Lines 16–20)

  • Line 16 – Federal tax withheld from W-2/1099 forms.
  • Line 17 – Any estimated tax payments you made.
  • Schedule 3 – Credits like education credits, retirement savings, and child tax credit.
  • Line 20 – Total payments and credits.
Extra Tips:
  • Credits reduce your tax owed directly.
  • Check every credit you qualify for — it can increase your refund!

🦉 Determine refund or amount owed (Lines 21–24)

  • If total payments > total tax → you get a refund.
  • If total tax > total payments → you owe money.
  • Line 24 – Refund amount (if any).
  • Line 23 – Amount you owe (if any).
Extra Tips: Always double-check numbers before submitting. Avoid surprises!

🦉 Sign and date your return (Line 25)

Line 25 – Don’t forget to sign and date your 1040 before submitting. Unsigned returns are rejected.

Extra Tips:
  • Both spouses must sign if filing jointly.
  • Keep a copy for your records.
