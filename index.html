✅ PART 1 — HOW TO DO TAXES (THE BIG PICTURE)

🦉 How to get ready for taxes

  • Find your W-2 (this shows money from jobs)
  • Get any 1099 forms (side jobs)
  • Check your bank account for interest earned
  • Write down any extra money you made
Extra Tips:
  • Put everything in ONE folder (paper or digital)
  • Don’t start until you have all your forms
  • If something is missing, ask early
  • Being organized makes everything easier

🦉 How to understand taxes (super simple)

  • Income = all the money you made
  • Deductions = money that lowers your income
  • Credits = money that lowers your tax (BEST one!)
Credits can give you money back 💰. Deductions only reduce how much is taxed. Focus on these 3.

🦉 How to fill out your tax form

  • Start at Line 1
  • Read what it asks
  • Copy numbers from your forms
  • Move to the next line
Extra Tips:
  • Go slowly—this is NOT a race
  • Never guess numbers
  • Use a calculator
  • If a line doesn’t apply, skip it

🦉 How to check your work (VERY IMPORTANT)

  • Re-read every line
  • Check your math
  • Make sure numbers match your forms
Extra Tips:
  • Most mistakes are small (like typing errors)
  • Double-checking can help you get a bigger refund
  • Take a short break, then check again

🦉 How to know if you get a refund or owe money

  • If you paid MORE than needed → 🎉 refund
  • If you paid LESS → you owe
Refund = your money coming back. Owing money is normal sometimes.

✅ PART 2 — STEP-BY-STEP TAX FILING GUIDE

🦉 Step 2 — Fill in Your Personal Info

  • Your name, address, Social Security number
  • Spouse info (if married)
  • Dependents (kids or others you care for)
Double-check every letter and number—tiny mistakes can slow things down!

🦉 Step 3 — Add Up Your Income (Form 1040 Lines 1–8)

  • Line 1 – Wages, Salaries, Tips (from W-2)
  • Line 2 – Taxable & Tax-Exempt Interest
  • Line 3 – Dividends (qualified & ordinary)
  • Line 4 – IRA Distributions
  • Line 5 – Pensions & Annuities
  • Line 6 – Social Security Benefits
  • Line 7 – Other Income
  • Line 8 – Total Income
Keep all forms handy. Double-check every amount!

🦉 Step 4 — Adjustments to Income (Schedule 1)

  • Line 9 – Adjustments (student loan interest, IRA contributions, self-employment expenses)
  • Line 10 – Adjusted Gross Income (AGI = Total Income − Adjustments)
Adjustments help lower your taxable income, paying less tax.

🦉 Step 5 — Deductions (Lines 11–12, Schedule A)

  • Line 11 – Standard Deduction or Itemized Deductions
  • Line 12 – Taxable Income
Pick whichever deduction is bigger to lower your tax.

🦉 Step 6 — Calculate Tax (Lines 13–15, Schedule 2)

  • Line 13 – Tax
  • Schedule 2 – Additional Taxes
  • Line 15 – Total Tax
Double-check numbers—accuracy matters!

🦉 Step 7 — Payments & Credits (Lines 16–20, Schedule 3)

  • Line 16 – Federal Tax Withheld
  • Line 17 – Estimated Payments
  • Schedule 3 – Credits
  • Line 20 – Total Payments
Credits reduce tax owed and can give refunds!

🦉 Step 8 — Refund or Amount Owed (Lines 21–25)

  • Compare Total Tax vs Total Payments
  • If Payments > Tax → Refund
  • If Tax > Payments → Amount Owed
  • Line 25 – Sign & Date
Signing is like turning in homework—unsigned forms don’t count!

🦉 Step 9 — Supporting Schedules & Forms

  • Schedule A – Itemized Deductions
  • Schedule B – Interest & Dividends
  • Schedule C – Business/Self-Employment Income
  • Schedule D – Capital Gains & Losses
  • Schedule E – Rental & Partnership Income
  • Schedule F – Farm Income
  • Schedule SE – Self-Employment Tax
  • Schedule 8812 – Child Tax Credit
  • Form 8863 – Education Credits
  • Form 8880 – Retirement Savings Contributions Credit
Complete these schedules before transferring numbers into your 1040.
