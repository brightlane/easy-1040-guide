🦉 How to File Your Taxes

Step 1: Gather Your Income Papers

We need to know how much money you made this year. Let’s get all your papers together!

  • W-2: Shows your salary from your job.
  • 1099: Shows freelance, interest, or dividend income.
  • Other income: Side jobs, tips, or rental money.
📝 Tip: Keep these in a folder so nothing gets lost.

Step 2: Gather Your Deductions & Expenses

These can lower the tax you owe.

  • Receipts for charitable donations.
  • Medical expenses (if you plan to itemize).
  • Education costs like tuition and books.
📝 Tip: Take photos or scan receipts for safety.

Step 3: Collect Personal Info

  • Social Security numbers (yours, spouse, and dependents).
  • Bank info for direct deposit.
📝 Tip: Double-check numbers — one mistake can delay your refund!

Step 4: Read Your W-2

Box 1 = total money earned. Box 2 = tax already paid. Box 3 = Social Security wages.

📌 Tip: Compare to your pay stubs to make sure everything matches.

Step 5: Add Up All Income

Combine W-2, 1099, and other incomes to see total money made.

📝 Tip: Don’t forget side jobs or bank interest!

Step 6: Choose Deductions

Pick standard deduction (easy) or itemize if your expenses are high.

📝 Tip: Use the option that lowers your taxes the most.

Step 7: Apply Tax Credits

Credits reduce taxes directly. Examples: Child tax credit, education credits.

📝 Tip: Make sure you claim all credits you qualify for.

Step 8: Calculate Tax

Subtract deductions and credits from income to see how much tax you owe.

📌 Tip: Use tax tables or software to avoid mistakes.

Step 9: Apply Payments

Include tax already paid from W-2 or estimated payments.

📝 Tip: This tells if you get a refund or owe money.

Step 10: Determine Refund or Amount Owed

If payments > tax owed → refund. If payments < tax owed → you owe money.

📌 Tip: Double-check numbers before submitting.

Step 11: Sign & Submit

Don’t forget to sign your 1040 — unsigned forms get rejected!

📝 Tip: Keep a copy for yourself.
