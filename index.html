✅ PART 1 — GETTING STARTED

Step 1 — Gather Your Papers

  • W-2 from your employer
  • 1099 forms for freelance or interest
  • Receipts for deductions (education, charity)
  • Last year’s tax return (if you have it)
🦉 Professor Hootington: Think of this as collecting all your school supplies before starting a project. You can’t work without them!

Step 2 — Fill in Your Personal Info

  • Your name, address, Social Security number
  • Spouse info if married
  • Dependents (children or others you claim)
🦉 Professor Hootington: Double-check every letter and number—tiny mistakes can slow things down.

✅ PART 2 — FORM 1040 LINES

Line 1 — Wages, Salaries, Tips

Copy Box 1 from your W-2 forms here.

🦉 Professor Hootington: Accuracy is key! Add multiple jobs if needed.

Line 2 — Interest and Dividends

  • 2a: Tax-exempt interest
  • 2b: Taxable interest
  • 3a: Qualified dividends
  • 3b: Ordinary dividends
🦉 Professor Hootington: Keep your forms handy—don’t guess numbers!

Line 4–8 — IRA, Pensions, Social Security, Other Income

  • 4a/4b – IRA distributions
  • 5a/5b – Pensions and annuities
  • 6a/6b – Social Security benefits
  • 7 – Other income (prizes, gambling, etc.)
  • 8 – Total income
🦉 Professor Hootington: Add carefully to get your total income correct!

Step 3 — Adjustments, Deductions, Credits, and Refunds

Follow IRS instructions for Schedule 1–3, choose deductions, calculate credits, and determine refund or amount owed.

🦉 Professor Hootington: Credits reduce your tax dollar-for-dollar. Deductions lower your taxable income. Both help you pay less!
