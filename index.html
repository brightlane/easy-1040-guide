1️⃣ Line 1 – Wages, Salaries, Tips

Enter total wages from all W-2 forms. Example: Employer A: $30,000 + Employer B: $10,000 = Total $40,000
💡 Tip: Check that your name & SSN exactly match your Social Security card.

2️⃣ Line 2 – Taxable Interest

Add taxable interest from banks or investments. Bank A: $50, Bank B: $150 = Total $200
💡 Tip: Exclude tax-free municipal bonds.

3️⃣ Line 3 – Dividends

Ordinary: $100, Qualified: $50 = Total $150
💡 Tip: Qualified dividends are taxed at lower rates.

4️⃣ Line 4 – IRA Distributions & Pensions

Traditional IRA: $2,000, Pension: $1,500 = Total $3,500
💡 Tip: Only taxable portions count.

5️⃣ Line 5 – Social Security Benefits

Check SSA statement. Example: $12,000
💡 Tip: Use IRS worksheet to find taxable amount.

6️⃣ Line 6 – Capital Gains

Stocks sold: Gain $500, Loss $200 = Net $300
💡 Tip: Short-term gains are taxed higher.

7️⃣ Line 7 – Total Income

Sum of Lines 1–6 = $40,000 + $200 + $150 + $3,500 + $12,000 + $300 = $56,150

8️⃣ Adjustments (Schedule 1)

Student Loan Interest: $1,000, IRA Contribution: $2,000 = Total Adjustments $3,000

9️⃣ Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)

Total Income $56,150 – Adjustments $3,000 = AGI $53,150

🔟 Deductions

Standard Deduction (Single) $13,850
💡 Tip: Itemized deductions only if greater than standard deduction.

1️⃣1️⃣ Taxable Income

AGI $53,150 – Deduction $13,850 = Taxable Income $39,300

1️⃣2️⃣ Tax (Use IRS Tax Table)

Taxable Income $39,300 → Tax $4,500

1️⃣3️⃣ Credits (Schedule 3)

Child Tax Credit: $1,000, Education Credit: $500 = Total Credits $1,500

1️⃣4️⃣ Total Tax

Tax $4,500 – Credits $1,500 = $3,000

1️⃣5️⃣ Other Taxes (Schedule 2)

Self-Employment Tax $300 = Total Tax $3,300

1️⃣6️⃣ Total Payments

Federal Tax Withheld $3,500 + Estimated Payments $0 = $3,500

1️⃣7️⃣ Refund or Amount Owed

Total Payments $3,500 – Total Tax $3,300 = Refund $200
