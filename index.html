🦉 Professor Owl’s Line-by-Line 1040 Guide (Beginner Version)

This guide breaks the 1040 form into simple steps so anyone can understand it.

📄 Step 1: Personal Info (Top of Form)

👉 Your name, address, and Social Security number.

Why it matters: This tells the IRS who you are.

💼 Step 2: Line 1 – Wages

Enter your W-2 income (job earnings).

Example: $25,000 from your job

🏦 Step 3: Lines 2–8 – Other Income

Includes interest, dividends, freelance, and side jobs.

Example: bank interest or 1099 income

➕ Step 4: Line 9 – Total Income

Add all income together.

Formula: Wages + Other income

🧾 Step 5: Adjustments (Lines 10–11)

Subtract things like student loan interest or retirement contributions.

Result: Lower taxable income

📊 Step 6: Line 11 – AGI (Adjusted Gross Income)

This is your “true income” after adjustments.

📉 Step 7: Line 12 – Deductions

Choose:

  • Standard deduction (most people)
  • Itemized deductions (if higher)

💰 Step 8: Line 16 – Tax Amount

This is how much tax you owe before credits.

🎯 Step 9: Credits (Line 19+)

Credits reduce your tax bill directly.

Example: Child tax credit, education credit

💳 Step 10: Payments (Line 25–33)

Shows tax already paid through withholding or estimates.

📦 Step 11: Final Result (Refund or Owe)

If you paid too much → refund

If you paid too little → you owe

🖊️ Step 12: Sign and Submit

Always sign your return or it is invalid.
