1️⃣ Line 1 – Wages, Salaries, Tips

This is the first line where you report your **total wages** from your W-2 forms.

Employer A: $30,000
Employer B: $10,000
Total: $40,000
💡 Tip: Double-check your **W-2** forms for **accuracy**, especially the **Social Security Number**.

2️⃣ Line 2 – Taxable Interest

Report the taxable interest from your **bank accounts, savings accounts, or bonds** on Line 2.

Bank A: $50
Bank B: $150
Total: $200
💡 Tip: **Exclude tax-free municipal bond interest** from this line.

3️⃣ Line 3 – Dividends

This line is where you report **dividends** that you received from stocks or mutual funds.

Ordinary: $100
Qualified: $50
Total: $150
💡 Tip: **Qualified dividends** are **taxed at lower rates** than ordinary dividends.

4️⃣ Line 4 – Taxable Refunds, Credits, or Offsets of State and Local Income Taxes

If you received a state or local tax refund last year, report it here only if you **itemized deductions** last year.

State Tax Refund: $500
Refund Reported: $500
💡 Tip: If you **did not itemize** in the prior year, **do not report** your state refund.

5️⃣ Line 5 – Alimony Received

If you received **alimony payments** in the past (for divorces finalized before 2019), report the total here.

Alimony Received: $2,000
Total: $2,000
💡 Tip: **Alimony** received for divorces finalized **before 2019** is taxable income.

➕ Additional Income

This section covers things like **self-employment income**, **rental income**, and more. For example:

  • Self-employment income: $10,000
  • Rental income: $5,000
💡 Tip: Keep track of **all sources of income** beyond your W-2 to avoid missing anything.

⚖️ Adjustments to Income

Adjustments reduce your total income. Some of the most common ones include:

  • Student loan interest: $2,500
  • IRA contributions: $3,000
💡 Tip: These adjustments are subtracted from your income to **lower your taxable income**.

💰 Additional Taxes

This section covers taxes that may apply to you, such as **self-employment tax**, and **additional Medicare tax**.

  • Self-employment tax: $1,500
💡 Tip: **Self-employment tax** applies to anyone earning money as a freelancer or business owner.

🎯 Tax Credits

This section includes tax credits, which directly reduce the amount of taxes you owe. For example:

  • Child Tax Credit: $2,000
  • Education Credit: $500
💡 Tip: **Tax credits** can be more valuable than deductions because they directly reduce your **tax owed**.

📬 Filing & Refund

Once all lines and schedules are complete, it’s time to **file** your return. You can file electronically or by mail.

  • Double-check your **W-2** forms, **social security numbers**, and **bank account information**.
  • Submit your return electronically or mail it to the **IRS** address.
  • Track your refund with the **IRS “Where’s My Refund?” tool**.
💡 Tip: **E-file** and **direct deposit** is the fastest way to get your refund.
